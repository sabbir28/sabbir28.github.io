Attack by Chhatra League on Quota Reform Activists at Shahjalal University

Quota reform activists are taking injured students with them due to the attack by Chhatra League.

By MD. Sabbir Hoshen Howlader

Country: Bangladesh

Activists of Chhatra League have launched an attack in protest against quota reform activists at Shahjalal University of Science and Technology in Sylhet. Three students were injured in the incident. The incident occurred around midnight on Sunday in front of the central library building of the university. In response to the attack, students announced a protest rally on campus on Monday.

The injured students are Delwar Hossen, a graduate student of the English department, Asadullah Galib, a graduate student of the Chemistry department, and Hafizul Islam, a third-year student of the Mathematics department, all admitted to Sylhet M.A.G. Osmani Medical College Hospital.

Criticism has been made for disrespecting quota reform activists in government jobs. Students took out a protest procession on campus around 11 pm on Sunday, declaring a protest rally. During the procession, they reached in front of the central library building where students shouted various slogans including 'We came for our rights, became Razakar'.

At one point, leaders and activists of Chhatra League took out a procession from Shahparan Hall with slogans like 'There is no place for Razakar in my golden Bengal'. At one point, the two sides faced each other in front of the central library building. Chhatra League leaders and activists tried to prevent the protesters from making a human chain. However, ignoring the obstacles of Chhatra League, the ordinary students tried to move forward. In response, Chhatra League leaders and activists beat several students.

Asadullah Al Galib, an injured student, alleged, 'We were conducting a peaceful protest on campus. We learned that some brothers were interested in coming to the procession of Chhatra League. So, we went to the hostel and the leaders and activists of Chhatra League obstructed our procession. At one point, they attacked.'

However, Chhatra League President Khalilur Rahman denied the allegations of attack. He first told the light, 'No incident of attack took place. There is video footage available to everyone, which will be understood. Rather, we went to the side of the road to ensure that their own departure.'
